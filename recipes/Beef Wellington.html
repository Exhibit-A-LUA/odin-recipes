Beef Wellington

Ingredients

  • 1.5 kg beef fillet, trimmed and tied
  • salt and black pepper
  • 2 tbsp sunflower oil
  • 45g butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 250g flat mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 175g smooth liver pâté
  • 400g ready-made puff pastry
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • gravy, to serve

Instructions

  1. Season the beef with black pepper.
  2. Heat the oil in a large frying pan, add the beef, and cook over a high heat until browned all over.
  3. Put the beef fillet in a roasting tin and cook in a preheated oven at 220C (200C fan, Gas 7) for 25 minutes for rare beef, 35 minutes for medium, or 40 minutes for well-done.
  4. Leave to cool completely.
  5. Meanwhile, melt the butter in the frying pan, add the onion and the mushrooms, and cook, stirring, for 3 minutes or until softened.
  6. Increase the heat to high, and cook until the excess moisture has evaporated.
  7. Turn into a bowl and leave to cool completely.
  8. Add the liver pâté to the mushroom and onion mixture, season with salt and pepper, and stir well to combine.
  9. To wrap the beef and pâté mixture in the pastry, roll out 300g (10 oz) of the pastry to a 30 x 40 cm (12 x 16 in) rectangle.
  10. Spread half of the pate mixture down the middle, leaving a 10 cm (4 in) border on each side.
  11. Then, remove the string from the beef and place the beef on the pâté mixture.
  12. Cover the beef with the remaining pâté mixture.
  13. Brush the pastry border with beaten egg.
  14. Fold the short sides of the pastry over the beef.
  15. Fold over the long ends and turn the pastry over.
  16. Brush with beaten egg.
  17. Roll out the remaining pastry, and cut into strips, 5 mm (¼ in) wide.
  18. Arrange in a lattice pattern on top of the pastry, then glaze the strips with the beaten egg.
  19. Bake at 220C (200C fan, Gas 7) for 45 minutes or until the pastry is crisp and golden.
  20. Cover with foil after 30 minutes to prevent the pastry becoming too brown.
  21. Leave to stand for about 10 minutes, then slice and serve with gravy.
