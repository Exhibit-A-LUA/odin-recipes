Masoor Dal Tadka

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 cups red lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 3 cups water or broth
  • 1 carrots, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1 green chilli, chopped
  • 1 tbsp curry powder (I use this one)
  • ½ tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, add lentils and water.
  2. Bring to a boil and simmer until lentils are fully cooked and tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  4. Add onions and garlic.
  5. Sauté until onions are translucent and garlic is fragrant.
  6. Add in carrots, onion, tomato, garlic, ginger, chilli, salt, and pepper.
  7. Sauté until carrots are soft.
  8. Then add in the curry powder, cumin, and turmeric.
  9. Add more salt and pepper, if needed.
  10. Use a spatula to combine and cook for another 2 minutes.
  11. Once the lentils are done, add in the cooked lentils and enough water as needed into the pan.
  12. Simmer over low heat for about 5 minutes, until the mixture is well combined.
  13. Stir in the coconut milk and simmer for 7 more minutes, or until dal is at your preferred consistency.
  14. Serve over freshly steamed rice and garnish with fresh parsley.
