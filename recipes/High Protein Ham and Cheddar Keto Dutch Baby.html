High Protein Ham and Cheddar Keto Dutch Baby

Description

Our high-protein Dutch baby is whipped up in a blender and takes less than 15 minutes to bake, making it simple enough for a hectic weekday morning. It is topped with smoked ham and shredded cheddar straight from the oven. Enjoy it hot or cold.

Ingredients

  • 160 ml heavy whipping cream, room temperature
  • 8 large eggs, room temperature
  • 55 (g) (55 ml) cream cheese, softened
  • 3 tbsp unflavoured whey protein isolate (unflavoured)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp onion powder (optional)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 230 (g) smoked deli ham, chopped
  • 180 ml (85 g) cheddar cheese shredded
  • 1 tsp unsalted butter, for the pan
  • 1 tsp dried chives or fresh chives (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the cream, eggs, cream cheese, whey protein isolate, baking powder, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt in a blender and blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Blend for at least a minute to aerate the mixture.
  4. Set aside.
  5. Put the butter in a 10" (25 cm) oven-proof skillet and place the skillet in the oven.
  6. When the butter begins to sizzle, remove the pan from the oven and pour the batter into the centre of the hot skillet.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the Dutch baby is puffy and browned.
  8. The centre should be just set.
  9. The Dutch baby is likely to rise in a lopsided fashion and will deflate somewhat as it cools.
  10. As soon as the Dutch baby is removed from the oven, top with the chopped ham and shredded cheddar cheese.
  11. Serve hot or cold.
  12. Garnish with fresh herbs such as chives, thyme, or parsley.

Tips

  • When warm, the texture of this dish is light, when cold it is more dense, like a custard.
  • If you aren’t familiar with a Dutch Baby, be prepared for it to bake up puffy and lopsided, and then to deflate just out of the oven.
  • For best results, be sure to let all ingredients come to room temperature before placing them in the blender.
