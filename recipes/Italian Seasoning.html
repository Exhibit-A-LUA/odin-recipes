Italian Seasoning

Description

Italian Seasoning. Making your own Italian seasoning is so simple you’ll wonder why you never did it before. Six flavourful herbs combined with just the right balance is the golden ticket. So versatile. Perfect for flavouring mayo or vinaigrette. Buon appetito!

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp dried basil
  • 3 tbsp dried oregano
  • 3 tbsp dried parsley
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • ¼ tsp chilli flakes
  • 1 tbsp sea salt (optional)

Instructions

  1. Add all ingredients to a bowl and stir until thoroughly blended.
  2. Pour into a jar with a tight-fitting lid.
  3. If you use whole seeds, grind in a grinder either in advance or when cooking.
  4. Keep the spices in a dark, dry and cool/room temperature place.
  5. Small tin cans are great.
  6. Make a big batch to last for 4–6 months; after this the spices will lose some flavour and colour.
  7. They won't go bad, but will just be less powerful.

Tips

  • If you don't have all the herbs above, you can either skip them or exchange for something else.
  • Marjoram works well instead of parsley for example.
  • We used dried garlic flakes, but you can also use garlic powder.
  • Adding salt, or not, to your spice mix is a matter of choice.
