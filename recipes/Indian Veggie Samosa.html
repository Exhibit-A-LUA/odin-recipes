Indian Veggie Samosa

Description

Indian Veggie Samosa

Ingredients

For the Samosa dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1⁄2 tsp salt
  • 1⁄4 cup oil or ghee
  • 1⁄3 cup water

For the Veggie Samosa filling

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped
  • 1⁄2 cup green peas, boiled
  • 4 potatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1⁄2 tsp asafetida
  • 1⁄2 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1⁄4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Oil, for deep frying

Instructions

Make the Samosa Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together all the dough ingredients except the water.
  2. Once combined, slowly add in the water and knead using your hands.
  3. Cover and set aside.

Prepare the Samosa Filling

  1. In a large saucepan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat.
  2. Add in the chopped onion, ginger, salt, and pepper.
  3. Sauté until onions are soft and ginger is fragrant.
  4. Add in the potatoes, carrots, and green peas.
  5. Cook until the vegetables are soft.
  6. Then add lemon juice and all the remaining spices, including more salt and pepper if needed.
  7. Once combined, stir in the chopped parsley and set aside.

Assemble the Samosa

  1. To make the samosa, take the prepared dough and knead it again until smooth.
  2. Divide the dough into 6 portions.
  3. Then roll each one into an oval shape, about 8-9 inch long and 6-7 wide.
  4. Cut each oval in the centre, dividing it into 2 equal “moon shaped” portions.
  5. Use water to seal the cut edges and join the edges to form each “moon shape” into a cone.
  6. Stuff the “cone” with the prepared samosa filling.
  7. Use water on the edges to seal tightly.
  8. Repeat with the remaining dough to make 12 samosas.
  9. For a less traditional shape, you can also use this handy press to assemble your samosas.

Cook the Samosa

  1. Deep fry the samosa on medium-low heat (allow it to fry undisturbed for about 10-11 minutes).
  2. Then turn up the heat to medium, and flip over the samosas.
  3. You’ll know it’s done when the samosas turn golden and crisp.
  4. Remove the samosas and transfer to a colander, or a plate lined with paper towels.
  5. Serve the samosas as is, with green chutney, or use it to make delicious Samosa Chaat.
