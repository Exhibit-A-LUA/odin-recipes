Sashimi Plate with Wasabi Mayo

Description

When it's too hot to cook, or if you're craving Japanese food, this beautiful sashimi dish steps up to the plate. Sushi-grade salmon is decorated with cucumber ribbons, radish slices, and dotted with spicy wasabi mayo. This low carb, higher-satiety meal is quick to put together and so satisfying to eat. Chopsticks at the ready!

Ingredients

  • 350 g salmon (raw sashimi grade)
  • 280 g cucumber
  • 4 radishes
  • 2 tbsp pickled ginger aka gari
  • ½ tbsp wasabi paste
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp tamari soy sauce
  • 1 tsp black or white sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix the wasabi with mayonnaise in a small bowl. Set aside.
  2. Slice the sashimi into 1/8-inch (3 mm) slices and arrange the slices on the plates.
  3. Cut the cucumber horizontally twice — not all the way through, it should stay intact on one end — so that you end up with three long cuts.
  4. Place the cucumber on your chopping board so that the cuts are vertical.
  5. Use a vegetable peeler to slice long, thin ribbons.
  6. Thinly slice the radishes.
  7. Arrange the veggies on the plates with the salmon.
  8. Top the plates with gari, a drizzle of tamari soy, and little dollops of wasabi mayo.
  9. Sprinkle with sesame seeds if using.

Tips

  • If you're not comfortable with raw fish, you can use cooked salmon, shrimp or tuna instead.
