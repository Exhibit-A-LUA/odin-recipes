Rainbow Cajun Salmon Skewers

Description

Rainbow Cajun Salmon Skewers

Ingredients

  • 900 g salmon, boneless fillets, cut into cubes
  • 1 tbsp light olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 lime juice and zest
  • 1Â½ tbsp cajun seasoning
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 (110 g) red onion
  • 1 (120 g) red bell pepper
  • 1 (140 g) yellow bell pepper
  • 1 (140 g) green bell pepper

Cajun Seasoning

  • see Cajun Seasoning Recipe

Instructions

On a grill

  1. Preheat the grill.
  2. Cut the salmon fillets into 1-inch (2.5 cm) cubes.
  3. Place the cubed salmon in a large bowl.
  4. Drizzle the salmon with olive oil, and sprinkle with minced garlic, lime juice and zest, cajun seasoning, and salt.
  5. Toss gently to combine.
  6. Set aside to marinate while preparing the vegetables.
  7. Chop the red onion into wedges and the bell peppers into 2-inch (5 cm) pieces.
  8. Thread the skewers starting with a wedge of red onion, followed by bell pepper pieces, and a piece of salmon.
  9. Repeat until the skewer is full.
  10. Grill the salmon skewers for 3-4 minutes on each side until just cooked through.

Oven broiler method

  1. Set the oven to broil on high.
  2. Rest the ends of the skewers on the sides of a baking tray or roasting pan.
  3. Place the baking tray about 6 inches (15 cm) from the heat source.
  4. Broil for 3-5 minutes, then remove the pan to turn the skewers over.
  5. Broil for another 3-5 minutes.
  6. For thinner pieces of salmon, 6-8 minutes total cooking time is enough.
  7. For thicker pieces, broil for 8-10 minutes.

Cajun Seasoning

  • see Cajun Seasoning Recipe

Tips

  • To achieve a rainbow effect, thread the veggies as follows: red onion, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, green pepper, and then salmon.

Special equipment

  • Using long, flat metal skewers makes it easier to turn the skewers on the grill.
  • If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for an hour before threading the salmon.
  • You can use two sticks per salmon skewer to make them easier to turn.
