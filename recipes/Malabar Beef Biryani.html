Malabar Beef Biryani

Description

Malabar Beef Biryani

Ingredients

Masala

  • 340 (g) beef/mutton
  • 2 tablespoon ghee
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 8 green chilli
  • 10 clove garlic
  • 1 inch piece ginger
  • 1 tablespoon chilli powder
  • 1⁄2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • salt and pepper
  • 3 tablespoons curd
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Water, as needed

Rice

  • 4 tablespoons ghee
  • 3 cups basmati rice, rinsed and drained
  • 6 cups water, or as needed
  • 4 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1⁄3 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 3 cinnamon sticks
  • 2 star anise
  • 5 cloves
  • 5 cardamom
  • 2 bay leaves

Garnish

  • onions
  • cashews
  • raisins
  • fresh parsley
  • lemon juice

Instructions

For the Meat

  1. In a large bowl, marinate the beef with turmeric powder, garam masala, garlic, ginger, salt and pepper for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  2. Add 1⁄2 cup water into a pressure cooker and cook until meat is tender, or about around 3-4 whistles.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare the masala.
  4. Grind the green chillies, garlic and ginger.
  5. Heat 2 tablespoon ghee in a large pan.
  6. Add in onions and salt and saute until golden brown.
  7. Add the ground green chilli mix and saute for another 1 minute, until fragrant.
  8. Add the chopped tomatoes and saute until soft.
  9. When your beef is done, transfer the beef and the liquids from the pressure cooker to the pan.
  10. Cook for 5 – 10 minutes, then taste and adjust the spices as needed.
  11. Add the lemon juice and curd.
  12. Mix with the masala and remove from the heat.
  13. Finally, add some chopped parsley and set aside.

For the Rice

  1. In a large pot, heat ghee on medium heat.
  2. Once hot, add in cinnamon, turmeric, bay leaf, cardamom seeds, cloves and star anise.
  3. Sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add in the rice, water, and salt.
  5. Bring water to a boil, then cover and cook until all of the water is absorbed and the rice is soft, about 15-20 minutes.
  6. Depending on the rice you use, you may need to add extra water as well.
  7. In a non-stick pan, heat ghee on medium-high heat.
  8. Add in the thinly sliced onions, then fry until crisp and golden.
  9. When the rice is done, remove the pot from heat, then fluff up the rice with a fork.
  10. Mix in the fried onions, along with cashews, raisins, lemon juice and fresh parsley.

To Assemble

  1. Option 1: In a large pan, mix the beef masala with the biryani rice together, along with lemon juice, fried onions, cashews, raisins, and fresh parsley. Serve with pickle and papad.
  2. Option 2: Layer a pan with alternating layers of rice and the prepared beef masala.
  3. Add lemon juice, chopped parsley, fried onions, cashews, and raisins at every layer.
  4. Heat the layered biryani for about 10-15 additional minutes.
  5. Serve with pickle and papad.
