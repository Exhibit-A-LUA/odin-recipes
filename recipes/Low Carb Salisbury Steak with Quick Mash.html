Low Carb Salisbury Steak with Quick Mash

Description

Salisbury steak may not be “real” steak, but who cares? It’s easy to make and so delicious. Served with cauliflower mash and a gluten-free onion gravy, this is low Carb comfort food at its best!

Ingredients

Cauliflower Mash

  • see Cauliflower Mash Recipe

Salisbury steak

  • 650 (g) ground beef
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tbsp coconut flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp ghee or butter

Onion gravy

  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter
  • 1 (110 g) yellow onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 240 ml beef broth or chicken stock

Instructions

Cauliflower Mash

  • see Cauliflower Mash Recipe

Salisbury Steak

  1. In a bowl, combine the beef, onion powder, egg yolk, coconut flour, salt, and pepper.
  2. Using your hands, create 3" (7 cm) patties, about 4.8 oz (135 g) from the mixture.
  3. Grease a large skillet with ghee or butter.
  4. Once hot, add the burgers.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium and cook for about 3 minutes on each side.
  6. Do not flip the patties too soon or they will stick to the pan.
  7. When done, set aside and keep warm.

Onion gravy

  1. Add the ghee to the pan where you cooked the patties.
  2. Add the onion and cook over medium heat for about 7 minutes, or until lightly browned and fragrant.
  3. Add the tomato paste, mustard, and coconut aminos, and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Pour in the bone broth, bring to a boil, then remove from the heat.
  5. Let the gravy cool down for a few minutes, then pour into a blender and process until smooth.

For serving

  1. Serve the cauliflower mash with the patties and pour the gravy on top.

Tips

  • Store the patties and cauliflower mash in separate containers in the fridge for up to four days.
