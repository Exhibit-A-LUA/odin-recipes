Summer Asparagus And Halloumi Salad

Description

Rich in vitamins A, C, and K, watercress is a real nutritional powerhouse. And, of course, it’s ultra-low in carbs. Serve it with a dressing made with healthy fats, and top with slices of warm, salty, grilled Halloumi — or, if you’re dairy-free, poached eggs.

Ingredients

Salad

  • 400 g green asparagus, woody ends removed
  • 300 g halloumi cheese (see tip)
  • 1 tbsp ghee or olive oil
  • 85 g (600 ml) watercress, or arugula lettuce
  • 85 g (650 ml) baby spinach
  • 200 g cucumber, spiralized or sliced
  • 140 g cherry tomatoes, chopped
  • 140 g avocados, sliced
  • salt and pepper

Pesto Dressing

  • see Pesto Dressing Recipe

Instructions

Salad

  1. Fill a saucepan halfway with salted water and bring to a boil over high heat.
  2. Add the asparagus and cook for 3 to 5 minutes, until crisp-tender.
  3. Drain and set aside.
  4. Slice the halloumi into about 1⁄2" (1 cm) slices.
  5. Grease a large skillet, or griddle, with the ghee and heat over medium-high heat.
  6. Add the halloumi and cook (in batches if needed) for 2 to 3 minutes per side, until lightly browned.
  7. Do not flip the Halloumi too early: let it crisp up to prevent it from breaking.
  8. When done, set aside and keep warm.
  9. In a large bowl, toss together the watercress and spinach.
  10. Arrange the cucumber, tomatoes, and avocado slices over the greens.
  11. Place the asparagus in the middle, and top with the grilled halloumi.
  12. To serve, drizzle the pesto dressing over the salad and season with salt and pepper, to taste.

Pesto Dressing

  • see Pesto Dressing Recipe

Tips

  • For a dairy-free substitution, replace the halloumi cheese with one large, poached egg, per serving.
  • A simple Mediterranean vinaigrette dressing can be substituted for the pesto dressing.
