Sautéed Chicken Livers with Creamy Cauli Mash

Description

If you are sceptical about eating liver but would like to give it a chance because it's such a cheap superfood, you really should give this a shot. This stew with chicken livers gets lots of flavour from onion and garlic and crispy, smokey bacon. Liver doesn't get any tastier than this!

Ingredients

Sautéed Chicken Livers

  • 475 ml water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice or cider vinegar
  • 450 (g) chicken livers, sliced to 1" (2.5 cm) pieces or boneless chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter, divided
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • 110 (g) bacon, chopped
  • 110 (g) (160 ml) yellow onions, sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 240 ml chicken stock or beef broth, divided
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 3 egg yolks
  • fresh thyme, for garnish
  • 622 (g) Cauliflower Mash

Cauliflower Mash

  • see Cauliflower Mash Recipe

Instructions

Sautéed Chicken Livers

  1. Combine the water and lemon juice in a bowl. Add the livers and refrigerate overnight. This helps remove any gamey, metallic taste.
  2. While the cauliflower is cooking, heat a skillet greased with half of the butter over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken livers, season with salt and pepper, and cook for 1 to 2 minutes, stirring frequently, until browned on all sides.
  4. Transfer to a plate with a slotted spoon.
  5. Add the remaining butter to the skillet.
  6. When hot, add the bacon and onion.
  7. Cook for 8 to 10 minutes, or until the onion is lightly browned and the bacon is crisp.
  8. Then add the garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
  9. Pour about 1/8 of the stock in a small bowl and add the egg yolks, mix together with a fork, and set aside.
  10. Add the rest of the stock and the tomato paste to the skillet, and bring to a boil.
  11. Add the chicken livers and cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
  12. Slowly drizzle the egg yolks into the skillet with the liver while stirring.
  13. Cook until the sauce thickens slightly.
  14. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  15. Garnish with the fresh thyme, and serve with the prepared cauli-mash.

Cauliflower Mash

  • see Cauliflower Mash Recipe

Tips

  • Let the livers and mash cool, then refrigerate for up to four days.
  • If you don't have a steamer, put the cauliflower in a bowl with a bit of water and microwave for about 10 minutes, or until soft.

Why the egg yolks?

  • The egg yolks are added to make the sauce creamier and rich.
  • You could skip them if you want to simplify the recipe.

Recommended special equipment

  • Steamer and blender.
