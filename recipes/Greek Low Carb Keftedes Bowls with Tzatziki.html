Greek Low Carb Keftedes Bowls with Tzatziki

Description

These flavourful lamb meatballs (keftedes) served on a crispy, fresh salad with tzatziki will make your taste-buds sing. If you’re not a huge fan of lamb, you can replace it with any other ground meat of choice such as beef or turkey.

Ingredients

Tzatziki with Lemon

  • see Tzatziki with Lemon Recipe

Greek salad

  • 140 (g) cucumber, sliced and halved
  • 200 (g) cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 85 (g) (140 ml) green bell peppers, sliced
  • 28 (g) (40 ml) red onions, sliced
  • 120 ml (75 g) feta cheese, crumbled
  • 28 (g) (50 ml) olives, pitted
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Keftedes (Lamb Meatballs)

  • 450 (g) ground lamb
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp pepper
  • 28 (g) (40 ml) yellow onions, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried mint
  • 1 large egg
  • 120 ml (55 g) almond flour or 2.5 tbs coconut flour
  • 1 tbsp ghee or butter, for greasing

Instructions

Tzatziki with Lemon

  • see Tzatziki with Lemon Recipe

Greek salad

  1. Place the cucumber, cherry tomatoes, green pepper, and red onion in a bowl.
  2. Add the feta, olives, and oregano.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil.

Keftedes (Lamb Meatballs)

  1. Combine all the ingredients for the meatballs in a bowl.
  2. Using your hands, form the mixture into 1" (2.5 cm) meatballs (about 33 (g)/1.2 oz each).
  3. Grease a large skillet with the ghee.
  4. Once hot, add the meatballs in a single layer.
  5. Cook for about 10 minutes turning with a fork until browned on all sides and cooked through, or until the meat reaches a temperature of 160°F (71°C).
  6. Remove from the heat.

For serving

  1. Portion the Greek salad into bowls, add meatballs, and serve with tzatziki on top.

Tips

  • Refrigerate the meatballs and tzatziki in separate airtight containers for up to four days.
  • If you want thicker tzatziki, you can squeeze out the liquid in the grated cucumber before mixing it with the other ingredients.
  • You could replace the almond flour with coconut flour.
  • Since coconut flour absorbs more liquid than almond flour, you only need one-third of the amount.
  • If you would like to make this recipe egg-free, replace each egg with 1 tbsp olive oil.
