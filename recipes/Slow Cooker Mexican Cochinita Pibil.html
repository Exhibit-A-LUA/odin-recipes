Slow Cooker Mexican Cochinita Pibil

Description

This traditional Yucatecan dish is cooked at a low temperature for several hours for an incredibly tender and juicy result. Cochinita Pibil gets its beautiful red colour and mild peppery flavour from the Achiote spice. Decorated with pickled red onion, this Mexican jewel is sure to please the eye and the belly.

Ingredients

Pork

  • 1.4 kg pork shoulder

Marinade

  • 120 ml orange juice
  • 180 ml lemon juice
  • 50 g achiote paste or Annatto Paste
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp coriander, ground
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar 5%
  • 1 tsp salt

Pickled onions

  • 2 (220 g) red onions
  • 180 ml lemon juice
  • 180 ml orange juice
  • 1 habanero pepper

Serving

  • 120 ml (8 g) fresh cilantro chopped

Instructions

Marinating

  1. To prepare the marinade, add the orange juice, lemon juice, achiote paste, cumin, coriander, garlic, oregano, white vinegar, and a bit of salt to a blender or a food processor.
  2. Process until the mixture is homogeneous. Set aside.
  3. Cut the pork into 1.5" (4 cm) pieces, place them in a container, pour the marinade and toss to fully coat.
  4. Cover the container and let it rest in the fridge for at least 6 hours or overnight for maximum flavour

Cooking

  1. Add the meat and the marinade to the slow cooker and rotate with tongs until pork is evenly coated with marinade.
  2. Cook on low for 8 hours or high for 4 hours.
  3. While pork is cooking, cut the red onions into strips and chop the habanero pepper.
  4. Put them in a glass container and pour the orange and lemon juice on top.
  5. Cover and set aside in the refrigerator until you’re ready to serve.
  6. Once the pork is finished, remove it from the slow cooker and let rest until it’s cooled down enough to handle.
  7. Use two forks to shred the meat.
  8. Add shredded pork back into the slow cooker with the juices and the marinade.

Serving

  1. Serve with pickled onions and chopped coriander on top.

Tips

  • If you want to prepare "tacos de cochinita pibil", you can have a look at our keto tortillas.
  • Pickled onions can be stored for up to 5 days in the fridge, and the meat for up to 4 days in the fridge, or 2 months in the freezer.

Special equipment needed

  • Blender or food processor.
  • Slow cooker.

Other ways to cook Cochinita Pibil

  • Once marinated, Cochinita Pibil is traditionally wrapped up in banana leaves, tied with a natural fibre cord, and then cooked for 4 hours in the oven at 325°F (160°C).
