Kofta Sheet Pan with Spicy Veggies

Description

This easy, hearty dish is a low Carb week-night saviour that makes excellent leftovers, too! The spiced kofta meatballs are oven-baked, where they simmer in a tomatoey sauce with garlic, bell pepper, and cauliflower. We serve this higher-satiety meal with tangy Greek yogurt for that irresistible hot-cold contrast. Magic? Perhaps. Delicious? Definitely.

Ingredients

Sauce and vegetables

  • 650 (g) cauliflower
  • 2 (280 g) green bell peppers or sivri pepper
  • 240 ml vegetable stock
  • 400 (g) (400 ml) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tbsp (adjust according to your preference) chilli flakes or pul bieber
  • 1 tsp salt

Beef Koftas (meatballs)

  • 900 (g) ground beef or ground lamb
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 (110 g) yellow onion, grated or finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 120 ml (8 g) fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp coriander, ground
  • ¼ tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Serving

  • 240 ml (180 g) Greek yogurt (0% fat)
  • 60 ml (4 g) fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a roasting tin or oven-proof dish with parchment paper.
  2. Cut the cauliflower and the green bell pepper into 1/2-inch (1 cm) slices.
  3. Keep the cauliflower leaves; cut them into smaller pieces and place them on the baking sheet with the cauliflower and bell pepper.
  4. Combine the vegetable stock, crushed tomatoes, minced garlic cloves, and salt in a bowl or large measuring jug and pour it over the vegetables.
  5. Stir to cover the vegetables with the sauce.
  6. Bake in the oven for 10-15 minutes while you prepare the koftas.
  7. Add the ground beef, eggs, yellow onion, garlic, parsley, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, salt, and black pepper to a bowl and mix until combined.
  8. Shape into meatballs, using about 1/3 cup (0.5 dl) of the mixture per kofta.
  9. Remove the vegetables from the oven and place the koftas on top of the vegetables.
  10. Brush with olive oil and bake for another 25 minutes or until cooked through.

Tips

  • Ground chicken or turkey would also work well in this recipe.
  • Sivri Pepper is a pepper used widely in Mediterranean cuisine. It is sweet, pungent, and its heat can range from mild to very hot.
  • Pul biber, sometimes known as Aleppo pepper, is a variety of crushed chilli flakes used in Turkish cuisine that has a fruity, smoky, and warm flavour.
