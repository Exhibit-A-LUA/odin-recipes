Egg Bhurji

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp butter (salted or unsalted, replace with oil for dairy-free recipe)
  • 80 grams chopped onions
  • 2 tsp finely chopped green chillies
  • 1 tsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1 tsp chopped garlic
  • 75 grams finely chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 0.25 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp pav bhaji masala
  • 0.5 tsp Kashmiri red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 2 tbsp chopped cilantro (fresh coriander leaves)

Instructions

  1. Whisk eggs and salt in a medium mixing bowl using a wire whisk. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil and butter in a pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Once the oil is hot, add onions and saute until the onions turn translucent, stirring frequently.
  4. Add green chillies, ginger, and garlic, and saute until the onions are light brown in colour Stir frequently.
  5. Now, add tomatoes and cook for another minute.
  6. Mash the tomatoes with the back of the ladle while cooking.
  7. Next, add coriander powder, turmeric powder, pav bhaji masala, and Kashmiri red chilli powder, and cook for 30-40 seconds, stirring continuously.
  8. Reduce the heat to medium-low.
  9. Add the whisked eggs to the pan.
  10. Stir the eggs using the ladle until they get mixed with the masala and gets scrambled.
  11. Note â€“ If you like soft bhurji, cook the eggs just until set.
  12. Remember that they will keep cooking for some time from the residual heat in the pan.
  13. If you like perfectly cooked bhurji, then cook until the eggs are nicely cooked.
  14. Add lime juice and cilantro and mix well. Serve hot.
