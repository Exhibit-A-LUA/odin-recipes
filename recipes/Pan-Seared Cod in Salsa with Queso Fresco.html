Pan-Seared Cod in Salsa with Queso Fresco

Description

Fish is an excellent source of protein that can be prepared quickly in a hot skillet. The cod is lightly seasoned, seared, and then simmered in salsa. Serve with a dollop of sour cream, fresh avocado, or crumbled queso.

Ingredients

  • 350 g cod, boneless fillets
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 120 ml chunky salsa
  • 60 ml sour cream
  • ½ (100 g) avocado
  • 55 g queso fresco or cheddar cheese

Instructions

  1. Sprinkle the chilli powder, cumin, and salt over both sides of the fish fillets.
  2. Heat the oil in a skillet over high heat.
  3. When the oil is hot, add the fish and sear for 1.5 minutes on each side.
  4. Reduce heat to low and pour the prepared salsa around the filets.
  5. Simmer the fish in the salsa for 3-4 minutes.
  6. Plate the fish, and pour the salsa evenly over the fish.
  7. Serve with sour cream, avocado, and crumbled queso fresco.
  8. Garnish with a wedge of lime and chopped fresh cilantro if desired.

Tips

  • No cod? Swap any mild white fish such as halibut.
  • Out of jarred salsa? A can of diced tomatoes (with or without chillies) can be used in a pinch.
  • Add your own Tex-Mex seasoning.
  • And if Queso fresco isn't a staple in your kitchen, use a cheese like cheddar, Gouda, or Monterey jack instead.
