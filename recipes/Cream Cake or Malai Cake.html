Cream Cake or Malai Cake

Description

I use the cream which comes on top of full cream milk after boiling and chilling in the fridge overnight. You can easily double the quantity for a larger tin and bake for about 10-15 minutes extra. If you don't have an egg beater you can still bake this cake. I have used the Indian measuring cup of 200 ml . You can use the American measuring cup for this too... just increase the baking powder to 1 teaspoon and use large eggs.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chilled cream
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup heapful flour (1 level cup + one fourth level cup to be very precise, if the concept of heapful is not clear to you)
  • 3/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 eggs

Instructions

  1. Gently beat the cream and sugar just until dissolved.
  2. Do not over-beat as sometimes the cream turns in to butter.
  3. That's one reason I have asked you all to take chilled cream.
  4. Another option is to just stir the two together and keep in the fridge for a little while so that they get dissolved.
  5. Take it out after half an hour or so, let it come to room temperature say in ten minutes and take the following steps.
  6. Throw in the two eggs and beat with a whisk. sift the flour with the baking powder and soda.
  7. Throw in the flour. I literally did that.
  8. Ideally one should add one spoon at a time and gently fold in, but I just threw in the whole lot of the flour and beat gently with the whisk.
  9. Pour the batter into a small tin and place in the centre of a preheated oven at 180 degree Celsius.
  10. Bake for 35 minutes or till a toothpick inserted comes out clean...maybe five minute more.
  11. Let the cake come to room temperature before taking it out of the tin.
