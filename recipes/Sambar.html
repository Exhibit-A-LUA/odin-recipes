Sambar

Description

Sambar - Serves 8

Ingredients

  • ½ cup Moong Dal (2.5 Cups Water)
  • 1 Eggplant
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 15-20 Small Onions
  • 6 large Bhindis
  • 2 green chilli
  • 1 Kerala Cucumber (Dosakaya)
  • 2 tbsp Sambar Powder
  • 1 Drumstick
  • 1 Large Potato
  • 1/2 tsp powdered methi seeds
  • 4 Arvis
  • Mustard Seeds
  • Tamarind lime-sized
  • 2 Whole Red Chillies
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • Curry leaves
  • 1 tsp Red chilli powder
  • Hing powder
  • ½ tsp Hing powder

Prep

  • Soak tamarind in water
  • Cut Potatoes, eggplant, tomatoes and cucumber into medium cubes
  • Cut Bhindis into half-fingers
  • Cut Arvis into quarters
  • Cut onions into quarters or halves
  • Cut drumstick into fingers and halve long-ways

Instructions

  1. Pressure cook dal in water.
  2. Meanwhile cook onions and sliced green chillis, hing, cucumber, potatoes, drumstick, add turmeric, arvi, red chilli powder, and salt
  3. Cover with water, medium flame, cover
  4. Meanwhile fry in coconut oil, bindi, eggplant, salt.
  5. After a bit add tomatoes, fry for a bit then add water and let cook
  6. Once Potatoes etc is half cooked, add tamarind water, medium flame
  7. Cover and cook for 5 mins,
  8. Add Eggplant etc, and the dal.
  9. Mix sambar powder in a little water, and add.
  10. Add curry leaves, coriander leaves (optional), jaggery (optional).
  11. Turn off when done

Temper

  1. Heat oil, mustard seeds, 2 red chillis broken, handful onions roughly chopped.
  2. Once onions are soft, add curry leaves, once onions are browned add powdered methi seeds and hing.
  3. Pour into sambar.
  4. Cover and cook for 5 mins, then mix through
