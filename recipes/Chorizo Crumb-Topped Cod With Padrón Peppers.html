Chorizo Crumb-Topped Cod With Padrón Peppers

Description

This high protein keto recipe provides the perfect combination of Mediterranean flavours Cod is baked and then topped with a Parmesan cheese and Spanish-chorizo crumble to enhance the flavour of the fish. As a side, Padrón peppers are the perfect match for fish dishes.

Ingredients

  • 50 g Spanish chorizo (hard and cured)
  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, shredded
  • 3 tbsp almond flour
  • 14 g butter
  • 400 g cod, boneless fillets
  • salt or ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 200 g Padrón peppers
  • 1 pinch coarse salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Shred the chorizo with a food processor, or dice it into small pieces with a knife.
  3. In a bowl, mix together the chorizo, parmesan cheese, and almond flour.
  4. Add the butter to the bowl and use your hands to mix the ingredients until it resembles crumbs.
  5. Place the cod into a baking pan lined with parchment paper.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and spoon the crumble mixture over the top.
  7. Bake for 10 minutes on the middle rack.
  8. After this, place the baking pan on the upper rack and bake for 5 more minutes until the crumble top is golden.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it rest.
  10. Keep it in the baking pan so the crumble hardens a little bit and the fish finishes cooking under its own retained heat.
  11. In the meantime, heat the olive oil in a pan over medium-high heat and add the peppers.
  12. Cook for 5 minutes stirring and turning occasionally.
  13. They should be golden but not burnt.
  14. Remove from heat and add coarse salt.
  15. Serve the cod with the peppers.

Tips

  • Choose mild chorizo if you do not want to hide the taste of the fish.
  • Choose spicy chorizo if you prefer a unique and stronger taste.
  • This recipe can be stored in the fridge for two to three days in an airtight food container.
